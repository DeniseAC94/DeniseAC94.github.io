Research network

Collaboration and support across disciplines

My work sits between academic research, practical methods support and public-facing science communication. I have contributed to projects involving biological shape, taxonomy, fossils, ecology, isotopes, museum specimens, applied reports and tutorial support for researchers working with morphometric data.

This includes formal research collaborations, student and researcher support, tutorial correspondence, consultancy-style report contributions, and international enquiries linked to geometric morphometrics and biological shape analysis.

Methods support

International morphology methods help

Through YouTube tutorials, research correspondence and Morphology Methods Hub, I have supported people working with landmark data, R workflows, PCA, Procrustes ANOVA, allometry, PGLS, SIBER and related biological shape analysis questions.

Collaboration themes

Shape analysis

Geometric morphometrics

Landmark-based studies of mammals, fishes, reptiles, fossils and anatomical structures using 2D and 3D biological shape data.

Mammals

Mammalian morphology

Collaborative work involving Carnivora, bats, bears, canids, rats and broader mammalian variation, including cranial and mandibular morphology.

Taxonomy

Fish taxonomy and morphology

Work on East African cichlids, Lake Malawi systems, Tanzanian riverine haplochromines and taxonomic questions involving morphology and species diversity.

Deep time

Fossils and palaeontology

Experience with fossil and palaeontological material, including morphology, zooarchaeology, pharyngeal tooth shape and deep-time questions.

Ecology

Applied ecology and isotopes

Contributions to stable isotope, fisheries, ecological and applied biological research, including report-based and collaborative projects.

Communication

Science communication

Public-facing science videos, tutorials and resources connecting evolutionary biology, morphology, fossils, speculative biology and research methods.
