Research & Publications

Research in morphology, evolution, fossils and biological shape.

My research focuses on morphological variation, geometric morphometrics, evolutionary biology, taxonomy, fossils, ecological patterns, and biological shape analysis across mammals, reptiles, fishes, invertebrates and fossil material.

Research highlights

PhD research

Morphological variation in mammals

My PhD research investigates morphological variation in mammals and ecogeographical patterns revealed through geometric morphometrics, with a focus on biological shape, size, ecology, geography and evolutionary pattern.

Geometric morphometrics

2D and 3D shape analysis

I use landmark-based approaches to analyse cranial, mandibular and anatomical shape variation across living and extinct organisms, including mammals, reptiles, fishes and fossil material.

Science methods

Practical analysis support

Through tutorials and Morphology Methods Hub, I create practical resources for geometric morphometrics, R workflows, PCA, Procrustes ANOVA, allometry, PGLS and related methods.
