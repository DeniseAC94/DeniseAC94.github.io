Enquiry form

Please use this form for research, methods support, collaboration, workshops, YouTube, media, sponsorship or Morphology Methods Hub enquiries.

Analysis support enquiries

If you are asking about morphometric analysis support, please include:

  • your research question;
  • the type of data you are working with;
  • software or R packages used;
  • what you have already tried;
  • what you are stuck on;
  • whether you need a call, written feedback, paid analytical work, collaboration, or methods/results support.

Please do not send confidential data, unpublished manuscripts, sensitive information, or large files until we have agreed the scope of support.

YouTube and science communication enquiries

If you found me through my Fallout science, speculative biology, evolution, fossils, or fictional creature videos, you are welcome to use the form for video suggestions, science questions, collaboration ideas, media enquiries, podcast invitations, or relevant sponsorship opportunities.

I may not be able to reply to every general message, but I do read suggestions and may use common questions to shape future videos or public-facing science resources.

Academic integrity

Morphology Methods Hub provides educational support, methods guidance, and analysis troubleshooting. I do not complete assessed coursework on behalf of students, fabricate interpretations, guarantee significant results, or replace formal academic supervision.

The aim is to help you understand your own data and methods more clearly.

Suitable enquiries

  • geometric morphometrics and biological shape analysis;
  • R workflows, geomorph, PCA, Procrustes ANOVA, allometry, PGLS or SIBER;
  • research collaboration or co-authorship discussions;
  • paid analytical work, report support, coding or methods support;
  • workshops, teaching, training or guest sessions;
  • science communication, YouTube, media or sponsorship enquiries;
  • Morphology Methods Hub resources and future products.

Before getting in touch

I genuinely enjoy helping students and researchers, and many of my tutorials have grown from real questions people have asked me over the years.

I have also learned the hard way that informal help can easily grow into unpaid research labour. A quick question can become code checking, analysis planning, interpretation, figure advice, methods/results writing, report support or repeated troubleshooting. In some past projects, that kind of input contributed to outputs that benefited others, but was not recognised through payment, authorship or acknowledgement.

Short, specific questions are welcome where I have capacity. More involved support may need to be handled as paid work or as a formal collaboration. If payment is not possible, I am open to discussing co-authorship or formal acknowledgement where my contribution is involved enough to justify it.

This is not meant to discourage people from contacting me. It is simply about keeping support fair, transparent and sustainable.
