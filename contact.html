Before getting in touch

I genuinely enjoy helping students, researchers and viewers, and many of my tutorials have grown from real questions people have asked me over the years.

I have also learned the hard way that informal help can easily grow into unpaid research labour. A quick question can become code checking, analysis planning, interpretation, figure advice, methods/results writing, report support or repeated troubleshooting. In the past, I have agreed to provide unpaid research support on the understanding that substantial contributions would be recognised through co-authorship or formal acknowledgement. In some cases, that recognition did not materialise, despite the time and specialist input involved.

Short, specific questions are welcome where I have capacity. More involved support may need to be handled as paid work or as a formal collaboration. If payment is not possible, I am open to discussing co-authorship or formal acknowledgement where my contribution is involved enough to justify it.

This is not meant to discourage people from contacting me. It is simply about keeping support fair, transparent and sustainable.

Enquiry form

Use this form for research, methods support, collaboration, workshops, YouTube, media, sponsorship or Morphology Methods Hub enquiries.

What to include in your enquiry

For research or methods support, please briefly explain your research question, the type of data you are working with, the software or R packages used, what you have already tried, and what you are stuck on. It is also helpful to say whether you are looking for a call, written feedback, paid analytical work, collaboration, or methods/results support.

For YouTube, media or science communication enquiries, you can use the form for video suggestions, science questions, speculative biology topics, Fallout science ideas, podcast invitations, collaboration ideas, media requests, or relevant sponsorship opportunities. I may not be able to reply to every general message, but I do read suggestions and may use common questions to shape future videos or public-facing science resources.

Morphology Methods Hub provides educational support, methods guidance and analysis troubleshooting. I do not complete assessed coursework on behalf of students, fabricate interpretations, guarantee significant results, or replace formal academic supervision. The aim is to help you understand your own data and methods more clearly.

Please do not send confidential data, unpublished manuscripts, sensitive information, or large files until we have agreed the scope of support.
